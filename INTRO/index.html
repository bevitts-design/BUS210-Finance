FINANCE FRAMEWORK

Three questions drive every valuation conversation.

BASIC QUESTIONS IN FINANCE

Cash flows, risk, and value today.

01
What cash flows are generated by the asset?
02
What risks are associated with those cash flows?
03
What is the value of the asset today?

Financial assets include bonds and stocks. Real assets include buildings, land, and machinery.

DISCIPLINE BOUNDARIES

Finance vs. accounting.

Accounting

Keeps track of what happened in the past to the firm’s money.

Finance

Uses historical figures plus current information to decide what should happen now and in the future.

AREAS OF FINANCE

Four doors into the field.

Investments

What securities to buy or sell? How should risk and cash-flow timing be managed?

Financial management

How should a business get, use, and repay cash? Which projects should it pursue?

Institutions and markets

How capital flows through banks, stock exchanges, and OTC markets.

International finance

Trade across borders, global operations, and currency risk.

UNCERTAINTY

Future cash flows are uncertain in timing and size.

Company-specific factors

Low influence, high predictability.

Economic conditions

High influence, high predictability.

Unforeseen events

Low predictability, potentially high damage.

Industry dynamics

High influence, lower predictability.

RISK

Risk is potential negative impact to value and cash flows.
