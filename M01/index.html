TVM Variables

The four variables driving TVM calculations.

PV

Present value: the seed, or what the cash flow is worth today.

FV

Future value: the harvest, or what the cash flow becomes later.

i and n

The interest rate controls speed. The number of periods controls distance.

Identify three variables, then solve for the fourth.

Excel Sign Convention

Think like a wallet.

Inflow (+)

Money received, such as a withdrawal or investment payoff.

Outflow (-)

Money invested or deposited, leaving your wallet today.

Excel requires you to define the direction of the cash flow.

Interest on Interest

Compound interest earns on the principal and accumulated interest.

Simple Interest

Earning only on the original principal.

Compound Interest

Earning on the principal plus accumulated interest.

Based on $100 at 7% for 3 years: interest on interest equals $1.50.

Simple Interest Calculation

Simple interest is linear.

Simple Interest = Amount × Interest Rate × n
$100 × 7% × 3 = $21
Total compound interest: $122.50 - $100 = $22.50
Interest on interest: $22.50 - $21 = $1.50

Compounding moves money forward. Discounting moves money backward.

Multiple Rates

Compounding when rates change over time.

Suppose rates increase from 7% this year, to 8% next year, to 8.5% in the third year.
FV = $1,500 × 1.07 × 1.08 × 1.085

Multiple Rates

Discounting when rates change over time.

What is the present value of a future $2,500 cash flow in year 3?
PV = $2,500 / (1.07 × 1.08 × 1.085)

The Rule of 72

A quick estimate for doubling time.

Years to Double ≈ 72 / Rate

Examples

3% rate: about 24 years.
9% rate: about 8 years.

Moving Cash Flows Through Time

Future value and present value are directions, not just destinations.

Moving it later

Compound forward: FV = $1,000 × (1.08)5 = $1,469.33.

Moving it earlier

Discount backward: PV = $1,000 / (1.08)3 = $793.83.

The formula must detect whether you are moving forward or backward.

Forward

Backward

If the target year is earlier, use a PV function.
