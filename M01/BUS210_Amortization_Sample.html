The trade-off: the lower payment costs far more. Choosing 30 years over 15 saves $447/month — but pays about $145,000 more in interest over the life of the loan.

Interest paid
Beginning balance × periodic rate
$200,000 × (6.40% ÷ 12)
= $1,066.67
Principal paid
Payment − interest
$1,251.01 − $1,066.67
= $184.35
Early in the loan, almost all of your payment is rent on the money — only about 15¢ of every dollar reduces what you owe.
